We buy and operate neighborhood laundromats in New York City.

Standard Laundry Partners is a small acquisition group focused on established laundromats across the NYC metro area. We look for durable neighborhood businesses with loyal customers, steady cash flow, and owners who may be thinking about a transition. Our goal is to buy good laundromats, operate them carefully, and continue serving the communities that rely on them.

What we look for

Geography

Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx, Manhattan, and Staten Island. We are especially interested in outer-borough stores with strong neighborhood density, repeat customer traffic, and room for thoughtful operational improvement.

Size

We are flexible on size. We look at single stores and small multi-store portfolios where the locations are well-run and locally established.

Store profile

We are most interested in laundromats with:

  • Verifiable cash flow
  • Stable lease terms
  • Established customer demand
  • Functional equipment and infrastructure
  • Room to grow service offerings
  • A strong neighborhood presence

We understand that every laundromat is different. Some stores are polished and fully modernized. Others have been operated the same way for years and may simply need new energy, systems, and hands-on attention.

Transaction structure

We can work directly with owners, families, brokers, landlords, and local advisors. We are flexible on timing, transition support, and deal structure. For owners who want to step away gradually, we are open to a thoughtful handoff period.

Who we are

Standard Laundry Partners is led by two partners with experience operating high-growth businesses. We are focused on buying and operating laundromats for the long term.

We care about practical businesses that serve real neighborhoods. Laundromats are local, essential, and relationship-driven. That is exactly why we like them.

For owners

Selling a laundromat is a personal decision. Many owners have spent years building their store, serving the same customers, managing staff, fixing machines, and keeping the lights on every day.

We respect that.

If you are considering retirement, simplifying your life, selling a family business, or just understanding what your store may be worth, we would be happy to have a confidential conversation. There is no pressure and no obligation.
